Proof contract

Keep the cryptographic promise narrow and useful.

nostr-veil gives applications a privacy-preserving threshold signal. The surrounding workflow decides what evidence, policy, expiry, and enforcement make that signal safe to use.

The proof verifies

  • Each contribution came from some member of the published circle.
  • Distinct contributors are counted once for the same circle, subject, and scope.
  • The published aggregate matches the signed contribution messages.
  • Proof v2 binds the assertion kind and subject hint.

The application decides

  • Circle admission, rotation, independence, and Sybil policy.
  • Evidence handling, appeals, reviewer criteria, and disputes.
  • Identifier canonicalisation for domains, packages, relays, and vendors.
  • Expiry, revocation, freshness, and incident response.

The verifier checks

  • Strict NIP-85 syntax before relying on the event.
  • verifyProof(event, { requireProofVersion: 'v2' }) for new subject-bound workflows.
  • The expected circle, threshold, metric meaning, and policy action.
  • Federation scope when multiple circles are combined.
